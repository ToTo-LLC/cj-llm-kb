01  ·  the thing itself

A second brain you can argue with.

brain is a local LLM-maintained wiki. You give it a folder of Markdown. It reads, classifies, files, and — when you ask — argues with you. Every change is staged. Nothing leaves your machine you didn't authorize.

This document is the brand's own small library. What brain looks like, what it sounds like, how it carries itself. If the product is a quiet, literate, trustworthy instrument, the brand should be too. Use this as a reference — not a cage.

02  ·  principles

Six commitments, in descending order of importance.

i.

Quiet over loud.

brain is not an AI futurist. No neural-network glow, no particles, no drop shadows pretending to be depth. Cream paper, ink, one warm accent. The work is what's interesting — not the wrapper.

ii.

Literal over clever.

Plain Markdown. Plain words. A wikilink is [[double brackets]]. A cost is a dollar figure. A risky action types its consequence. If we can say a thing plainly, we say it plainly.

iii.

Staged before applied.

Every visual decision — every approval surface, every destructive copy, every change of state — is drawn in the shape of the product's core loop. Propose. Review. Approve. The brand inherits that rhythm.

iv.

Durable over trendy.

A serif from the 1500s. A grotesque from 2016. A monospace made for code. Cream and ink. Nothing about this should look like 2026 and only 2026. It should survive a Finder folder in 2031.

v.

Honest about limits.

We ship with a known-issues document. We never claim what we haven't built. The brand does the same: empty states are honest, error copy tells you what failed and why, and nothing performs a confidence it doesn't have.

vi.

Yours, not ours.

Your vault is yours. The brand should feel the same: present, useful, gettable — then out of the way. When you read your own notes, brain's chrome should recede.

Proportion

A good brain surface is ≥80% paper, ~15% ink, ≤5% ember. If you feel the ember too loudly, it's too loud.

Dark mode

Ink becomes the surface. Paper becomes the text. Ember warms up one step (#C65C3F) to stay legible. Everything else stays muted.

State colors

Sage, wine, dusk are muted by design. They're signals, not alarms. Error is wine, not red. Success is sage, not green.

Voice — by example

Say this

"Nothing leaves your computer except the LLM API calls you configure."

"Every claim cites a file. Every citation is a real line you can click through."

"If brain shuts down tomorrow, you don't lose your knowledge. You lose the assistant."

"Type DELETE-VAULT to confirm. This cannot be undone."

"Your vault is yours. Forever."

Not this

"Unleash the power of AI on your knowledge base!"

"Our cutting-edge neural engine understands your notes."

"Seamlessly supercharge your productivity with AI-driven insights."

"Oops! Something went wrong. Please try again 🙈"

"Revolutionize how you think."

Rules of thumb

The name

Always brain, lowercase, even at the start of a sentence. Never Brain, never BRAIN, never brain™.

Destructive actions

Say what it will do, in present tense, with the consequence. Not "Are you sure?" — "This deletes 4 files. This cannot be undone."

Uncertainty

Name it. "The vault doesn't have enough to answer that." is stronger than an invented answer — and on-brand.

No emoji

Except rarely, in release notes, as punctuation — ✓ approved, ✗ rejected. Nowhere else.

Monospace speaks

Use it for the literal voice of the machine: paths, costs, commands, wikilinks, typed confirmations. Not for emphasis — we have italic for that.

Admit the limit

Every release ships with a known-issues document. The brand copy ships with the same honesty.

07  ·  motion

Motion is a promise the system is still with you.

Never decorative. Motion in brain means something happened, is happening, or is about to. It's always short (≤240ms for UI, ≤600ms for state) and it's always linear in its meaning even when it curves in its easing.

Arrival

160ms · ease-out · opacity + 4px lift

New patches, toasts, results. A soft arrival, never a slam. If you can't tell it moved, it was right.

Thinking

indefinite · a cursor, not a spinner

The LLM thinking is a blinking ember cursor, not a spiraling wheel. The work is inside; the surface stays still.

Approval

240ms · ease-in-out · tick + collapse

A patch approved collapses into the vault with a small sage check. Rejected patches fold down with no sound.

09  ·  the name, carefully

On calling a thing "brain".

The word brain is a generous target. It risks sounding clever (a common disease of AI products), generic (there are many brains in the world), or clinical (a thing with lobes). We guard against all three the same way: by never dressing the word up. No neurons in the logo. No synapses. No pulsing cortex glow. No "powered by neural AI."

The word's strength is its plainness. A brain is where you already keep things. brain is a tool for that. The dot after the wordmark — . — is the product's only flourish: a full stop, the end of a sentence, the opposite of hype. It says: this is finished. Use it.
